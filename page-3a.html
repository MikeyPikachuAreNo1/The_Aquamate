Deaf people and Synchronized Swimming

Did you know?

Deaf people can't compete in the Paralympic Games because the Paralympics don't have a category for athletes who are only deaf. Instead, deaf people compete in the Deaflympics. There are many sports competed in Deaflympics but synchronized swimming is not included in this event. There are also not many people with hearing impaired get involved in this sports. WHO estimates that there are around 466 million people worldwide with disabling hearing loss. They generally rely on lip reading or sign language for daily activities. World Deaf Day

World Deaf Day is celebrated every last Sunday of September, falling on September 28 this year. Why do we celebrate World Deaf Day? We celebrate World Deaf Day to raise awareness about the challenges and achievements of the Deaf community. It's a day to recognise their rich culture, unique language, and the importance of inclusion in society.

With both Summer and Winter editions, the Deaflympics follows a similar format to the Olympic Games, with athletes competing for medals and representing their countries. The games not only highlight athletic excellence but also the power of sports to bridge communication gaps and foster global solidarity.
